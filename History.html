History of "Love at First Sight"

  • Greekkk Times

  • The Greeks are often known as the origin of what we know as modern depictions of "love at first sight,” though as with all things with no known exact beginning, the emotion was likely around for much longer. Greek god Eros (later known as Cupid by the Romans) shot arrows that drove mortals crazy with love, and the myths were filled with stories of individuals falling in love often immediately.

  • Romanticism

  • About a century after Shakespeare’s influential tragedies filled with love and heartbreak, the Romanticism Movement expanded and brought a large increase in modern “romances” in theater. It was during this time that some of the first cases the term, or very related dictation of the term, “love at first sight” was used.

  • Modern Times

  • Today, “love at first sight” stories are everywhere, and the term has become a widespread cultural topic millions know of and follow. The romance genre is one of the most best-selling genres ever, and with help of modern media and the vast spread of knowledge throughout society, has taken over the way we base our own relationships with others.
